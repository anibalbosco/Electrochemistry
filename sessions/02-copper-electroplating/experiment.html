Session 2 — Experiment: Copper Electroplating

Session 2

Prep — Clean the cathode (25–35 min)

Object options

Steel nail, screw, coin, washer, or key

Procedure

  1. Sand or steel-wool the surface until visibly bright
  2. Rinse with water; pat dry
  3. Do not touch the plated area — hold by head/thread only
  4. Weigh or photograph before plating (optional, for comparison)

Quality checklist

  • [ ] No visible rust or grease
  • [ ] Uniform scratch pattern from sanding
  • [ ] Rinsed and dry

Setup — Plating cell (35–50 min)

Figure 1 — Electrolytic cell: Cu anode (+) dissolves; Cu²⁺ deposits on the nail cathode (−). Optional resistor limits current.

Procedure

  1. Pour ~50–100 mL copper solution into jar/beaker
  2. Copper anode → positive (red) terminal of 9 V battery
  3. Object to plate → negative (black) terminal
  4. Optional: 100–500 Ω resistor in series to limit current
  5. Immerse both electrodes; do not let them touch
  6. Measure current with multimeter in series (mA range) if possible

Plating run (50–70 min)

  1. Start timer when circuit is connected
  2. Check every 1–2 minutes: color, shine, roughness, bubbles
  3. Plate for 2–10 minutes (stop earlier if deposit turns black/powdery)
  4. Disconnect; rinse object gently; dry
  5. Compare to unplated control

Troubleshooting

Problem Cause Fix
No plating Reversed polarity Swap terminals — object must be (−)
Black/powdery Cu Current too high Add resistor; increase distance
Patchy deposit Dirty surface Re-sand and rinse
Anode not dissolving Wrong anode material Use copper anode
Battery gets hot Short circuit Check wiring; add resistor

Safety during experiment

  • Goggles on when working with solutions
  • Do not ingest or touch solutions bare-handed
  • Avoid short-circuiting 9 V battery
  • Collect copper waste separately
